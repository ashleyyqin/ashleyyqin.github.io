$4,320. That’s how much money the average college student wastes every year paying for rent when they’re away on break. Though most students only need housing for at most 9 months out of the year, high demand and limited options allow landlords to keep leases at 12-months long in many college towns. Operational difficulties — such as needing to clean before and after a guest stays in your home — have made home-sharing out of reach for most college students. ReRent was created to take care of all these operational hassles and help students save money on housing.

Launched in 2018, ReRent has successfully booked out 1000+ total nights and has served 400+ guests. However, all prior services were handled manually — from listing on Airbnb to booking cleaners. Furthermore, ReRent had only been operating in the Yale area as there was no infrastructure to allow for expansion to other schools. FINISH BACKGROUND.

Problem

How can I make ReRent scalable so we can help more students make the most of their housing? What infrastructure is needed to make home-sharing entirely painless for students?

Solution

Build a web application to be able to reach a wider customer base. Make interactions seamless and familiar to help students help themselves.
